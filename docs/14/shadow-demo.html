What You're Seeing
Directional: uniform parallel light (like the sun) — the whole ground is lit and only object-cast shadows appear.

SpotLight: cone-shaped beam — notice the dark rim at the edge of the cone, which can look like a shadow but isn't one.

With shadows OFF: all surfaces are lit with no depth testing — notice how flat and ungrounded the scene looks.
