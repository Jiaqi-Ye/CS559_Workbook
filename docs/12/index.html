Page 12: Fancy Shading

Three.js provides two different physically based lighting models. They provide a wider range of effects, and their parameters more closely match the actual properties of materials. Here is an example of a MeshPhysicalMaterial where you can experiment with the parameters.

physical.html

A big advantage of the physical materials is that they allow for image-based lighting. Basically, rather than making individual light sources, we can give a picture of the environment and use that as a light source. We will come back to this. But for now, you can experiment with what it does.

physical-hdri.html

We won’t discuss the physical lighting models much. You can use them without understanding the details: the intuitions from Phong lighting apply.

Before we can discuss image-based lighting, we need to talk about how we use images more generally. This brings us to textures.
