Workbook 3: Curves

In this workbook, we’ll get beyond straight lines to consider curves in 2D.

This workbook will teach you about how we represent curved shapes in 2D. The focus is on parametric curves (we’ll explain what that means), but you will see other approaches to curves in Computer Graphics (that are mainly useful for surfaces in 3D). We’ll start with some underlying mathematics that we need to talk about curves and then use those to build some useful curve forms. We’ll look at the most common types of curves used in Computer Graphics.

The workbook ends with a substantial programming assignment, the Train, and a “creative” assignment after that. But be aware: there are smaller required programming assignments and type-in boxes throughout. You can keep track of your work by using the checkboxes on the boxes and rubrics.

Required Reading/Watching

As an experiment, this year I am not asking you to read the textbook chapter. I don’t think it is as effective a way of learning the material as the visual approach in the videos and workbook. I can say that because I wrote the chapter. I have adapted the textbook into a set of web pages: Curves Tutorial. The Curves Notes are optional.

My favorite tutorial on curves is the amazing video The Continuity of Splines. It is over an hour long, but it covers an amazing amount of material in a very compelling way. The author is a designer and game developer who put an amazing amount of effort into creating animated demonstrations. I recommend watching it first. You won’t get the details, but you’ll be exposed to the concepts and intuitions. A warning: she covers things in a different order than I like to teach them. (She does Bezier curves first, I prefer to introduce them later.) She also mentions some topics we won’t get to in class. This video is required.

There is a shorter video focused just on Bezier curves (The Beauty of Bezier Curves) - I recommend watching that one later, when you get to the web pages on Bezier curves (I’ll put a reminder there).

Learning Goals

  1. Understand the types of curve representations and how to select among them.
  2. Understand curve parameterizations, tangents, derivatives, and continuity conditions.
  3. Understand piecewise polynomial representations of curves and the use of different polynomial forms.
  4. Understand the concept of interpolation and the tradeoffs in approaches to implement it.
  5. Understand the ideas and properties of Bezier curves including their geometric and algebraic formulations
  6. Understand arc-length parameterizations and how we approximate it.
  7. Understand practical issues in using curves in practice.
